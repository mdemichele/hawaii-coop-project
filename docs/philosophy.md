## Why Cooperatives?

There are a number of pressing questions that I think cooperative models could help us solve.

How do we give people in Hawaii more forms of ownership? How do we incentive individuals to care more about their local communities? And how do we do so in a way that respects place? In a way that is not simply another implementation of individualistic capitalism? Western capitalism, with its emphasis on private ownership and extraction, is in large part what destroyed Hawaii. How do we build new forms of ownership that reject this paradigm? That turns us back to Indigenous wisdom. To the wisdom of the Kanaka Maoli. How do we see through new - or rather, very old - eyes? How do we build shared responsibility into our thinking about ownership? Why can't we try other methods beyond corporate domination?

These are the types of questions that co-operatives may help us solve. At their heart, co-operatives seeks to ask, "what can we accomplish together that we cannot accomplish alone?" I want to make it easier for members of the community to build great things together. The work of building is fundamentally an act that ought to be done together. Our technologies ought to make this coming together easier. Instead, our technologies often separate us. They disconnect. They push us into silos. It is high time that we push back. We ought to build technologies that give us more tools for collaboration, for earth stewardship, and for a way back to shared responsibility.

How do we build technologies that simply draw us together and then get out of the way, allowing us space for the in-person collaboration we are sorely lacking?
